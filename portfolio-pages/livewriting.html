Writing Process Research

WatchMeWrite and Intrepid

WatchMeWrite

The written word is an asynchronous form of communication in which static texts are exchanged. However, writing is a dynamic process in which evolving ideas are organized into a coherent narrative. This process is not shared with readers because they only see the final product. In this paper, we aim to explore the potential benefits of watching a real-time replay of writing that reveals this writing process. We conducted an online survey (n=78) to compare reading comprehension and readers’ perception of watching writing replay with that of reading static text. We found that some styles of writing enhanced user engagement and the perceived quality of a text. In addition, we recorded the writing process of professional writers (n=13) and interviewed them about their and others’ replays. These writers found replays universally engaging. They facilitated self-reflection and helped writers empathize with each other. Participants from both studies considered using replay regularly.

Intrepid

Intrepid is a word processor designed for creative writing students who struggle with writer's block. In order to encourage rapid and freespirited drafting, Intrepid introduces a number of experimental features into a bare-bones word processor.

In Race the Clock mode, students are forced to maintain a certain writing pace (measured in Words Per Minute) in order to prevent having everything they've written deleted. In Focus Mode, all text but the sentence currently being written is hidden from the student. In Speak Aloud mode, every word the student writes is run through speech synthesis, therefore assosciating the student's work with the already familiar realm of speech.
