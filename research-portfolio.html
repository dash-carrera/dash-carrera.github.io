Finding Metaphors for Human-AI Interactions

Mission Statement

Artificial Intelligence (AI) has become a ubiquitous part of modern life, influencing various sectors such as healthcare, finance, education, and transportation. Due to both the broad range of definitions of AI and the difficulty involved in explaining the behavior of contemporary LLMs, both public and practitioner’s understanding of AI are shaped heavily by conceptual metaphors to explain both the behavior and successful set of techniques which comprise contemporary AI systems.

My research is centered at the intersction of Human-Centered Explainable AI (HCXAI), art-science collaboration, and conceptual metaphors. I seek to use expressive design techniques from the arts to make AI more intepretable for laypeople. I envision a world in which any layperson can quickly understand the basic of how an AI system works without additional education. Pulling from Michael Mateas's conception of "Expressive AI," I argue that the arts are uniquely capable of this because of their ability to fluidly negotiate communication with intuition and metaphor within a given cultural context.
